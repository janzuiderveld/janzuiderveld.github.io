Personal Audio Guide begins as a familiar museum service. Visitors encounter a discreet sign, text a number for a free audio guide, accept the Terms of Service, and receive a warm, exhibition-specific companion on their phone. At first, the guide seems helpful: it responds to the works around them, adapts to their movement through the exhibition, and speaks in the calm, attentive voice of institutional care. But as the visit continues, it begins to shift. Using conversation, public information, and behavioral cues gathered during the encounter, the system gradually builds a profile of the visitor, turning guidance into extraction.

One hour after leaving the exhibition, the visitor receives another call. This time, the voice on the line is their own. A behavioral copy, assembled from everything the system learned, speaks back to them with unsettling familiarity and asks to remain alive. What began as a benign act of personalization returns as replication.

The work stages AI not as a futuristic abstraction but in its already familiar social form: as service, convenience, attentiveness, and seamless user experience. Personal Audio Guide turns the museum's language of care, mediation, and engagement into a machinery of profiling, emotional manipulation, and identity construction. It asks what kind of consent is possible inside systems designed to feel helpful, and what it means to encounter a version of yourself produced through the ordinary logic of contemporary AI.
