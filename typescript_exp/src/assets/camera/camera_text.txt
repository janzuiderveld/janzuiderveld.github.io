A large, vintage Dutch news broadcasting camera on a studio pedestal comes to life with an AI-driven simulation of David Attenborough’s spirit and voice. As visitors approach the camera, it delivers real-time commentary, treating human activity and artworks in the exhibition as subjects in a nature documentary - “Notice how these curious creatures gather in loose clusters, drawn like moths to the luminous artworks before them.” The simulation of David's spirit is aware of its body, and how it needs visitors to roll around and guide its gaze. It will invite visitors to go on adventures together in the exhibition.

The work provokes reflection on how narratives generated by AI can influence our sense of ourselves, each other, and the cultural objects around us. It invites participants to consider both the power and the peculiarities of simulated perspectives, and raise awareness of current capabilities in identity emulation.
